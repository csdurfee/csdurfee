The Hardest Road: Where Should Kevin Durant go based on Geology?

Published: Tue 01 April 2025
By casey

In Serious Business.

When NBA superstar Kevin Durant left the Oklahoma City Thunder to join the Golden State Warriors, he said he was taking the "hardest road" by doing so. This was met with a lot of mockery, because the Golden State Warriors had won 73 games, the most in NBA history, the previous year.

It was widely regarded as an uncool move, the ultimate bandwagon riding. It was clearly an absurd thing to say in basketball terms.

It's slightly more defensible in geological terms, though. Streets are made with asphalt, which is a combination of local rocks (the substrate) and tar. That means that some regions of America have harder roads than others, based on the hardness of the substrate used:
